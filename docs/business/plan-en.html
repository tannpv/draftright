1 What is DraftRight?

A writing assistant. You highlight any text, choose a tone, and DraftRight instantly rewrites it — clearer, more professional, or in whatever style you need. It works inside email, messaging, documents, social media — anywhere you type.

You wrote

"hey can u send me the report asap thx"

→
DraftRight · Professional

"Hello, could you please send me the report at your earliest convenience? Thank you."

9 Cash flow & payback

  • Year 1: cash flow negative — $4,000 in, no revenue for 6 months. Normal for a software launch.
  • Month 9–10: operating break-even (~25 customers cover monthly costs).
  • Month 16–18: the full $4,000 is paid back.
  • From Year 2: steady, growing profit — customers keep paying monthly and new ones join.

10 Risks (straight talk)

🎯 Getting users is the hardest part. The product is finished; the challenge is marketing, not building.
🆓 Free built-in AI exists (Apple, Google). Our edge: every platform, more control, privacy, the Vietnamese keyboard.
⚖️ Intellectual property: I built this while employed and my employer doesn't know. We must check with a lawyer (~$50–100) before transferring ownership into the company.
💪 Solo founder — one person doing everything; burnout risk.

Maximum downside: lose the ~$4,000 capital. No loans, no debt.

In one paragraph: DraftRight is a finished AI writing app that works on every device and earns money through a monthly subscription. The product costs almost nothing to run, so the profit margin is ~90%. We invest ~$4,000 to register the company, give the app away free for 6 months to build users, then start charging. Year 1 is a small loss; the capital comes back around mid-Year 2; from there it earns steadily and grows. The most we can lose is the $4,000 — with no debt.
